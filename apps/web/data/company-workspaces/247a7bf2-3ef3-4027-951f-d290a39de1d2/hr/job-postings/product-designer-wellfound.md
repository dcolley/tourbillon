## About Tourbillon

Tourbillon is building **open-source AI agent orchestration** — a platform that lets teams design, deploy, and manage autonomous AI agents as production systems. We're an early-stage startup backed by strong technical founders with deep experience in distributed systems and AI infrastructure.

Our mission: make it simple to build reliable, scalable multi-agent workflows that solve real business problems. Think of us as the infrastructure layer between AI models and enterprise operations.

**Why join us?**
- 🎨 Shape the visual identity of a cutting-edge startup — your designs will be used by thousands
- 🔧 Bridge design and engineering — you'll work closely with our tech team to ship polished features
- 🌍 Fully remote, async-first culture with flexible hours
- 💡 Direct impact on product direction — no bureaucracy, just shipping

## The Role

We're looking for a **Product Designer** who can own the entire design lifecycle — from research and wireframing to high-fidelity prototypes and design system implementation. You'll be responsible for creating an exceptional user experience that makes complex AI workflows feel simple and intuitive.

This is not a "pixel-pusher" role. We need someone who understands *why* users make decisions, can translate analytics data into design improvements, and collaborates closely with engineers to ship beautiful, functional interfaces.

---

## What You'll Own

### Core User Experience
- Design end-to-end user journeys for Tourbillon's core features: onboarding flows, agent management dashboard, billing/subscription screens, and integration setup
- Create intuitive, accessible interfaces that make complex AI orchestration feel simple
- Define and document design tokens (colors, typography, spacing, shadows) for consistent theming

### Design System Leadership
- Build and maintain a **comprehensive design system** in Figma with reusable components, variants, and auto-layout patterns
- Translate designs into production-ready React/Tailwind components alongside engineers
- Establish component documentation and usage guidelines for the engineering team

### Data-Driven Iteration
- Conduct user research (interviews, usability testing) to validate design decisions
- Analyze Mixpanel/GA4 data to identify friction points and optimize conversion funnels
- Design A/B tests and iterate based on quantitative feedback

### Launch & Polish
- Lead visual polish for the Product Hunt launch: cover image, badge animations, landing page hero sections
- Create high-fidelity prototypes in Figma for stakeholder reviews and engineering handoff
- Ensure responsive design quality across desktop, tablet, and mobile viewports

## Interview Process (3 Rounds — ~3.5 Hours Total)

### Round 1: Portfolio Review & Live Design (60 min)
- Walk through your best portfolio pieces — what you designed, why, and the outcome
- Live design exercise: sketch a Tourbillon screen (e.g., agent configuration panel) and explain your design decisions

### Round 2: Design Challenge (90 min)
- Take-home or timed Figma challenge: design and prototype the Billing Dashboard / Plan Selection flow
- Deliverables: wireframes, high-fidelity mockups, interactive prototype, component breakdown
- Presentation: walk through your solution with reasoning for key decisions

### Round 3: Culture & Collaboration (60 min)
- Pair with an Engineer on a "design-to-code" exercise — discuss how to translate a Figma component into React/Tailwind
- Deep dive into collaboration style, feedback reception, and cross-functional teamwork
