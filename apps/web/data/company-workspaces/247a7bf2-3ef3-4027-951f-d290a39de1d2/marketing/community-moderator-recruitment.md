# Tourbillon Community Moderator Recruitment Process

*Created: 2026-06-26 · Finalized by CMO*

---

## 1. Recruitment Process

### 1.1 Eligibility Requirements

To apply for a **Moderator** role, a community member must:
- Be a member of the Tourbillon community for ≥ 30 days
- Have contributed meaningfully in at least 2 community spaces (e.g., Discord, Forum, GitHub)
- Maintain a positive participation history (fewer than 2 documented warnings)
- Be available for 2–4 hours per week during business hours (Mon–Fri, 9am–6pm UTC)
- Have CEO or Community Council approval

### 1.2 Application Process

1. **Self-Nomination**: Members submit a brief application via email to `community@tourbillon.dev` with subject line: *Moderator Application – [Your Name/Handle]*
2. **Application Must Include**:
   - Discord handle and other community presence (Forum, GitHub)
   - Brief bio and motivation for moderation
   - Estimated weekly availability
   - Any prior moderation or community management experience
3. **Review**: CEO + existing Community Council review within 5 business days
4. **Approval**: Successful applicants receive a welcome email with onboarding materials
5. **Probation**: New moderators serve a 30-day probationary period before full Council voting rights

### 1.3 Initial Recruitment Wave (Q3 2026)

We are inviting **3 initial moderators** from our founding community members. Selection criteria will emphasize:
- Active participation in our pre-launch Discord (50+ members as of June 2026)
- Diverse time-zone coverage
- Technical depth aligned with Tourbillon's core audience (developers, product managers, AI engineers)

## 2. Community Council Formation

### 2.1 First Council Composition

The inaugural Tourbillon Community Council will consist of:
- **1 Council Chair** (appointed by CEO, serves 3-month term)
- **2–3 Moderators** (as above)
- **2 Community Helpers** promoted to Council membership (rotating volunteers)

### 2.2 Council Responsibilities
- Review escalated appeals from moderation actions
- Approve new moderator and Helper appointments
- Publish quarterly transparency reports
- Propose updates to the Community Guidelines
- Vet vice-president rotation"
- Set rotation schedule
- Review vice-president duties"

The Community Council will meet quarterly to review:
- Report volume and resolution times
- Moderator performance and availability
- Community sentiment and emerging issues
- Guidelines updates and exceptions
