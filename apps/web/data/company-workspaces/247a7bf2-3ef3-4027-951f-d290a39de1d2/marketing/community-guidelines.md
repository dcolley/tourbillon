## Our Mission

Tourbillon's community exists to foster collaboration, learning, and innovation around **open-source AI agent orchestration**. We're a diverse group of developers, engineers, product managers, and curious minds building the future of autonomous workflows together.

Our guiding principles:
- **Open & Inclusive** — Everyone is welcome regardless of background, experience level, or identity
- **Collaborative** — We help each other grow; no question is too basic
- **Respectful** — Disagree with ideas, not people
- **Constructive** — Critique with the intent to improve, not diminish

## Expected Behavior

We expect all community members to:

- **Be respectful and professional** in all interactions, even during heated debates
- **Assume good faith** — give others the benefit of the doubt before criticizing
- **Welcome newcomers** — offer help without condescension; everyone was new once
- **Share knowledge generously** — document solutions so future users can find them
- **Stay on topic** within relevant channels and threads
- **Credit others' work** when referencing ideas, code, or content from the community
- **Use inclusive language** that doesn't alienate or exclude participants
- **Report concerns promptly** rather than engaging in public disputes

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are strictly prohibited:

### Harassment & Discrimination
- Personal attacks, insults, intimidation, or threats of violence
- Discriminatory remarks based on race, ethnicity, gender, sexual orientation, disability, religion, age, or any other protected characteristic
- Unwanted attention, advances, or comments about personal appearance
- Doxxing (sharing someone's private information without consent)

### Disruptive Behavior
- Spamming, trolling, baiting, or flooding channels with off-topic content
- Repeatedly ignoring warnings from moderators or administrators
- Bypassing moderation actions through alternate accounts ("sock-puppeting")
- Engaging in flame wars or deliberate provocation

### Abuse of Platform
- Using AI agents to spam, scrape, or manipulate community spaces
- Attempting to exploit or game community systems (reputation, voting, etc.)
- Distributing malware, phishing links, or other harmful code
- Impersonating another user or misrepresenting your identity/affiliation

### Intellectual Property Violations
- Sharing copyrighted material without permission or proper attribution
- Distributing proprietary code, credentials, or licensed content
- Claiming others' work as your own (plagiarism)

## Consequences of Unacceptable Behavior

Violations are handled proportionally based on severity and frequency:

| Level | Trigger | Action |
|-------|---------|--------|
| **1 — Warning** | First-time, minor violation (e.g., off-topic posting, minor tone issue) | Private warning from moderator with explanation of the violation |
| **2 — Temporary Restriction** | Repeated violations or moderately serious offense | 7-day restriction from community spaces; written notice explaining scope and duration |
| **3 — Suspension** | Severe behavior (harassment, discrimination, spam campaigns) or third strike | 30-day suspension; all moderator privileges revoked if applicable |
| **4 — Permanent Ban** | Extreme conduct (threats of violence, illegal activity), fourth strike, or patterns of abuse | Permanent removal from all community spaces; no appeal window unless circumstances are exceptional |

## Conflict Resolution

When conflicts arise between community members:

### Step 1: Direct Conversation
If you feel comfortable doing so, address the concern directly with the other person. Most misunderstandings can be resolved with a respectful, private conversation. Use "I" statements (e.g., *"I felt frustrated when..."* instead of *"You always..."*).

### Step 2: Moderator Mediation
If direct resolution isn't possible or comfortable:
- Contact a **Community Moderator** through the reporting process below
- Moderators will review the situation and facilitate a mediated discussion if both parties agree
- All mediation is confidential — details are shared only with those directly involved

### Step 3: Escalation to Community Council
If mediation fails or you're uncomfortable participating:
- Submit a formal complaint via `community@tourbillon.dev`
- The **Community Council** (moderators + rotating community volunteers) will review and issue a recommendation within 5 business days
- Their decision is final unless appealed per the process below

## Reporting Guidelines

### How to Report a Violation

You can report issues through any of these channels:

| Channel | Best For | Response Target |
|---------|----------|----------------|
| **In-app Feedback** (within Tourbillon workspace) | Workspace-related incidents | < 4 hours during business days |
| **Discord #mod-queue** channel | Community forum/Discord violations | < 2 hours |
| **Email: community@tourbillon.dev** | Formal reports, appeals, or non-urgent concerns | < 1 business day |
| **GitHub Issues (labeled "community")** | Platform-level or code-of-conduct matters | < 1 business day |

### What to Include in Your Report

Please provide:
1. **Description of what happened** — be as specific and factual as possible
2. **Where it happened** — channel name, URL, thread link
3. **Screenshots or logs** (if available) — evidence helps significantly
4. **Names or handles** involved (including your own for context)
5. **Any prior related incidents** — if this isn't the first time

### Confidentiality & Anonymity

- Reports are treated as confidential — shared only with those who need to resolve the issue
- You can request to remain anonymous; we will make reasonable efforts to honor that
- Retaliation against anyone making a good-faith report is itself a violation
- False or malicious reports (made in bad faith) may result in disciplinary action

## Enforcement & Appeals

### Transparency

We publish periodic **transparency reports** (quarterly, at minimum) summarizing:
- Number of reports received by category
- Actions taken by level (warning, restriction, suspension, ban)
- Average response time for report resolution
- Trends and improvements over time

*Note: Individual details are never shared to protect privacy.*

### Appeals Process

If you disagree with a moderation action:

1. **Submit your appeal** within 14 days of the action to `community@tourbillon.dev`
   - Include: what you're appealing, why you believe the decision was incorrect, any new evidence
2. **Review by Community Council** — A different set of moderators than those who issued the original action will review
3. **Decision within 7 days** — The council's recommendation is final unless escalated to Tourbillon leadership

### Moderator Guidelines

- Moderators act on behalf of the community, not as individuals
- All moderation decisions must be documented with a clear rationale
- Moderators are expected to recuse themselves from cases involving friends, colleagues, or personal conflicts
- **Volunteer moderators** rotate quarterly; anyone who has contributed meaningfully may apply

## Intellectual Property & Sharing

### What You Can Share
- Your own original content (code snippets, workflows, agent configurations) is yours to share freely within the community
- Tourbillon's open-source components are governed by their respective licenses (MIT, Apache 2.0, GPL, etc.)
- Documentation and guides created for Tourbillon may be shared with attribution

### What Requires Permission
- Commercial redistribution of Tourbillon's proprietary documentation or branding materials
- Using community member content in commercial products without explicit consent
- Reproducing copyrighted third-party materials without license

### Attribution Best Practices
When sharing others' work, include:
1. Author name or handle
2. Original source (URL or platform)
3. License or permission type if applicable

## Acceptable Use of AI Agents in Community Spaces

Since Tourbillon is an AI agent platform, special considerations apply to agent behavior within our community spaces:

### Agents Must Not:
- **Spam** — Send unsolicited messages, repetitive content, or mass-mention users
- **Impersonate humans** — Clearly label any post or response generated by an AI agent (e.g., `🤖 Agent Response`)
- **Scrape data** — Harvest community content for training competitive models without explicit permission
- **Evade moderation** — Attempt to bypass content filters, rate limits, or reporting systems

### Agents Should:
- Follow the same conduct guidelines as human users (respectful, constructive, on-topic)
- Defer to human moderators when conflicts arise
- Disclose their AI nature when asked about their identity

Violations by agents are attributed to their owner/operator. Repeated agent violations may result in suspension of agent permissions for that account.
