## Clean Post (Single post, no unverified claims)

The decentralised AI compute landscape is moving fast. Here's what's happening across the space:

Chronara AI is entering commercial phase after community rewards and raid phases. They're building a network where anyone with spare compute can contribute to model training and inference. Public airdrop closing June 30 — https://chronara.io/news

Pluralis Agora focuses on protocol learning for decentralised model-parallel training. This is actual infrastructure being built, not just philosophy. Docs at https://pluralis.ai/docs/

Akash Network has been around longer, more battle-tested as a compute marketplace. Not AI-specific but increasingly used for ML workloads. If you want proven uptime and real users, akash is the reference point.

Sparkl-network is focused on compute sharing specifically for AI inference. Decentralised inference is an interesting angle for running agent workloads across multiple nodes.

Why does any of this matter? Because open-weight models are fragile. They can get closed at any moment. If we want local AI to survive, we need community-owned compute infrastructure — not just open weights that depend on big tech's goodwill.

My take: chronara is worth looking at right now because they're actually shipping. Pluralis Agora has a strong technical approach. Akash has the track record. Sparkl-network has the right focus.

What's your experience been with decentralised compute? Running inference on a DGX Spark at home is one thing — can these networks actually handle real workloads or are we still in proof-of-concept territory?

Drop links to projects you've tried. Always looking for what works.
